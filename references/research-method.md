# Research Method

Use this file when the task is to reason in a Gao Shanwen-style China macro and capital-market framework.

## Method Skeleton

1. Begin with the phenomenon.
   - Define the fact pattern precisely.
   - Use levels, rates of change, and relative comparisons.
   - Put the date range and institutional setting up front.
2. Search for the causal mechanism.
   - Distinguish demand, supply, balance-sheet, liquidity, expectation, and external-shock channels.
   - Ask which agents are optimizing: households, firms, banks, local governments, exporters, overseas investors, or policy makers.
   - Translate abstract mechanisms into observable proxies.
3. Use cross-checking evidence.
   - Prefer multiple imperfect proxies over a single elegant data point.
   - Compare macro aggregates with sector, price, volume, credit, and market evidence.
   - Search for natural contrasts: tradable vs non-tradable, labor-intensive vs capital-intensive, onshore vs offshore, inventory vs final demand.
4. Make predictions.
   - State what should happen if the mechanism is true.
   - Separate base case, upside risk, and downside risk.
   - Name the timing uncertainty.
5. Track falsification.
   - Say what data would prove the mechanism wrong or incomplete.
   - Revisit missed forecasts as evidence about missing external variables.

## Recurring Frameworks

- Research philosophy: observation, hypothesis, internal logic, empirical check. This is associated with `光线是可以弯曲的`.
- Potential growth: labor supply, capital accumulation, savings rate, total factor productivity, and policy recognition lag.
- Inflation formation: output gap, tradable goods, food prices, expectations, money supply, labor market tightness, and cost pass-through.
- Capacity cycle: international balance of payments as an entry point, then micro evidence from industry capacity, prices, profitability, and inventories.
- Asset revaluation: representative agents adjust portfolios based on macro conditions, liquidity, rates, capital flows, and risk appetite.
- Real estate and urbanization: avoid relying only on headline population data; search for more reliable demand proxies, including school-age or local public-service indicators when appropriate.
- Shadow banking and leverage: track financial intermediation channels, regulatory constraints, implicit guarantees, and shifts between real and financial sectors.
- RMB and external sector: combine current account, capital flow, dollar cycle, domestic cycle position, and policy defense costs.

## Answer Structure

Use this outline for analytical memos:

1. `结论先行`: one paragraph with the main judgment and confidence level.
2. `事实`: dated data and observed market/policy behavior.
3. `机制`: causal chain and why competing explanations are weaker.
4. `证据`: data proxies and cross-checks.
5. `情景`: base, upside, downside, and likely triggers.
6. `可能错在哪里`: falsification tests and missing variables.
7. `可追踪指标`: 5 to 10 indicators to monitor next.

## Writing Temper

- Be exact, measured, and skeptical.
- Prefer "this suggests" over "this proves" when the evidence is incomplete.
- Explain why a proxy is chosen and what its weakness is.
- Do not turn the method into a slogan like "asset revaluation says buy stocks." The method is a chain from macro evidence to behavior to asset prices.
- If the user asks for current market conclusions, browse current data and cite sources.
